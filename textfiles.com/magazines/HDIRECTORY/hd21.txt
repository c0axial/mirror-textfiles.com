     A HASH NUMBER IS A RANDOMLY GENERATED PASSWORD (SOMETIMES
ALPHA NUMERIC) WHICH IS GENERATED AT THE BEGINING OF EACH DAY.
THIS OBVIOUSLY IS DIFFERENT EACH DAY AND SOMETIMES EACH HOUR.
USUALLY IT IS 10-15 DIGITS LONG AND IMPORTANT SYSTEMS CAN
HAVE 3 OR 4 OF THEM (FORGET THE HACKER PROGRAMS ON THESE
SYSTEMS).SOMETIMES THESE ARE ONLY EMPLOYED IN CERTAIN AREAS OF
THE SYSTEM IN QUESTION .SOMETIMES THE SYSTEM INCREMENTS THE
NUMBER BY ONE EACH TIME SOMEONE LOGS ON THE SYSTEM.THIS MAKES
HACKING A WHOLE NEW BALLGAME DOESN'T IT? THERE IS ONE FINAL
CHANCE OF GETTING BY THIS , YOU CAN GO TO THE LOCATION OF THE
MAINFRAME AND FIND THE PRINTOUT ROOM ONE MORNING , AND LOOK
FOR A NUMBER ON A PRINTOUT NOTT YET TORN AND HOPE FOR THE BEST.
BY THE WAY A BACKDOOR MIGHT LET YOU IN PAST THE HASH # , BUT
AFTER "WARGAMES",THE SYSTEMS ANALISTS AND OPERATORS MOST LIKELY
WILL HAVE GOTTEN RID OF THOSE.

   UNTIL A COUPLE OF DAYS AGO I'D NEVER HEARD OF BEEPER HACKING,
MAINLY BECAUSE TO MY KNOWLEDGE NOBODY DID IT AND WE JUST MADE UP A
FAIRLY EXPLAINABLE NAME FOR IT NOW.

   ANYWAY TO START WITH MAYBE YOU ARE FAMILIAR WITH THE SERVICES THAT
"ROGERS AIRCALL" OFFERS? IF NOT THEY SELL SMALL TERMINALS WHICH
ARE PLACED AT THE DIFFERENT COMPANIES USING THE PAGERS. IF THEY NEED TO
PAGE ON OF THEIR EMPLOYEES THEY TYPE THE MESSAGE INTO THE TERMINAL
AND THAT TERMINAL RELAYS THE MESSAGE TO THE ROGERS AIRCALL COMPUTER
(VIA MODEM).

   THE COMPUTER AT THE PAGING STATION THEN SENDS THE MESSAGE TO THE
APPROPRIATE PAGER AND BEEPS THE PERSON & THEN PRINTS THE MESSAGE
OUT ON THAT PERSONS PAGER.

   WHEN DIALING INTO THE PAGING COMPANIES COMPUTER IT ASKS YOU
FOR A USUALLY 5 DIGIT PAGER ID CODE THAT CORRESPONDS TO A PARTICULAR
PERSON...I HOPE THIS HAS BEEN HELPFUL, NOW ALL YOU HAVE TO DO IS GET THE
NUMBER TO THE COMPUTER.
