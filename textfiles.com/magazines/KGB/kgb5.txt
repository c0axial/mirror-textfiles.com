**************************[ 2. SCAN SCAN SCAN!@#$ ]**************************
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

 Well, the title says it all. Why? Because you never know what you'll find
 in your own area code. Here are some recommendations..........

  1. First, try to determine what prefixes would even be worth your computer's
     time for scanning. Try to scan in prefixes with plenty of offices and
     such. You'll hopefully find a few lines to some computers or whatever
     to try hacking. Or maybe you'll find some other high-tech things to
     connect with. Don't bother with residental areas and such that would
     most likely have plain folks like you living in. Only scan there if
     there's nothing else to scan in your AC.

  2. Try this on for size when summer's over. Setup your computer to start
     scanning right before you go out the door. Have it scan whatever prefix
     you want(more on that later). When you get home, I bet you'll more than
     likely have some carriers to call and play with when you get home. There,
     you just got some new 0-day carriers to hack and explore into when you
     were sleeping in class. Ain't that great? It's like an after-school snack
     for your brain. You might strike it big and hit an untouched system 75
     (yeah right) or something even more k-rad that I can't even describe.

  3. This may sound nerdy, but try to organize a scanning group. Yeah, you
     heard me. A k-rad hacking group dedicated just to have peoples' computers
     dial number after number after number seeking those other single and
     available modems for that perfect match. If you want to be even more
     elite, get yourself an acronym or something (to keep your members feeling
     important and happy). Assign each club member to scan a certain prefix
     (or a group of prefixes). You can find a list of prefixes in your phone
     book. And as soon as all your carriers are rounded up after a certain
     period of time, compile them all and give 'em out to the whole group.
     Have your little group meetings take place on BBSes, and password zip
     the result files (or better yet EMAIL them) to the other members, and go
     on from there. Maybe a carrier that's a total mystery to you might go
     way back with another d00d in the group. Basically, just pool the
     information, and soon you'll know what every carrier to find in your AC
     and be k-rad.

     {This is a pretty good idea, actually. A friend of mine had the same
     idea a few days ago while blabbering in a bbs post... - PaX}

  4. If your area code already has that Caller ID shit or whatever, just use
     that elite *67 shit. You know how those anal-retentive fuckers will
     call you back if they have some dude calling them and not saying a word,
     then hanging up.

  5. No matter how pointless or fruitless of a carrier you may connect to,
     KEEP IT LOGGED. It might provide valuable clues to something else
     someday (just hopefully not to the feds :), and if you can't hack into it
     today for whatever reason, you might learn how to hack it some other day.

  6. If you are completely bored out of your mind, just sit back and listen
     to the modem (make sure it's turned on first) and see what your
     computer's dialing. If you *know* it's not a carrier, hit whatever key on
     your wardialer's program to cycle to the next number. NO sense in wasting
     valuable scanning time (yeah right). Who knows, you might find some
     PBX or whatever for you to write down while a scan is in progress.
     What I do is just hook up my k-rad phone dubber unit to my tape recorder
     and output the audio thru my Labtec speakers. I can hear it much
     better than with my pussy-ass modem speaker.

     {After KFC wrote this, I remembered that I had a good wardialer program
     that I had obtained a long time ago. I edited the source and made it
     really simple to use. One thing tho, you won't be able to see anything
     when you type the #s in. It takes as many characters as it needs. I
     have it ask you if what you entered is correct so that you can go back
     and change it if you made a mistake. It even lets you put a k0d3 in
     before dialing the number (like *67,xxx-xxxx). The scanner is added in
     the .ZIP archive. If you have any problems, email my buddy
     phigan at 15660@ef.gc.maricopa.edu - PaX}
