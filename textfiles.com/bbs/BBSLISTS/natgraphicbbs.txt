        HOW TO USE:  It's easy.  The first  column  gives  U.S.  area
        codes; a period denotes Canadian entries.  The second  column
        gives the GUI format the BBS host uses.  And the last  column
        gives additional information.  A slash has been used for each
        "flag" to  help make string searches easier.  At the  end  of
        the file you will find the Flagging Information, GUI Terminal
        Emulators, Email  Contact Addresses and Entry  Change  Credit
        lists.  Read on for more information, explainations and news.

        Purpose:  Although many GUI (graphic user interface) BBS sys-
        tems have been put online - there tends to not be many of the
        same  format  located in local calling areas.  This  list  is
        ment to help those in the United States and Canada find BBSes
        of the GUI terminal type they want.

        Quality:  We  can't do it alone, this list needs  YOUR  help!
        Please  take  the time to call numbers local to you  in  your
        area code and see if they are still active.  Busy signals  or
        carrier can be assumed ok; ring or voice means it's down.  If
        all folks using this list would take a moment to verify  num-
        bers local to them on occassion, the quality of this database
        would be worthy indeed of your continued interest.

        Distribution:  We  always will need circulation of this  list
        to  any  and all public access systems so  GUI  BBSes  become
        known  and  other  potential systems  are  encouraged  to  go
        online.  Please upload anywhere & everywhere!

        Submissions:  Look  to  the end of this file  to  locate  the
        Flagging and Contact Lists.  Mark your entry with the  appro-
        priate flags, and then send to a network contact address that
        you have access to.  Credit will be given in the next  issue.


        * The author of VGIX (/VG) will no longer be supporting
          that format, but will be converting to RIPSCRIP (/RS).
          Please help us.  Sign-on to the VGIX systems local
          to you and let us know if they have changed formats.

        * The author of ROBO (/RB) will no longer be supporting
          that format, but will be converting to ROBO-FX (/FX).
          Please help us.  Sign-on to the ROBO systems local
          to you and let us know if they have changed formats.

        * We are seeking names of all GUI terminal programs,
          along with the systems they run on and the format they
          can display.  Please send what information you can to
          a contact address at the end of this file.

        * EXCALIBUR (/XC) is a new format joining the list.
          It uses a Windows based GUI terminal. Check it out.

        * Lastly, thanks go to all the authors, sysops and users
          who are listed near the end of this file.  They are
          the ones who make your dialing easier and more enjoyable.
