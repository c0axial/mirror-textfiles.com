

Name: Police Positive, Police Positive Target, Police Positive
Target Model G and Police Positive Target Model C.

Identifying features: fixed sights; topstrap matted to reduce
glare; removable left sideplate; Positive Lock.

Calibers (inc. variations): .32-20, .32 Short Colt, .32 Long
Colt, .32 Colt New Police, .32 S&W short, .32 S&W Long, .38 Colt
New Police, .38 Special, .22 Long Rifle

Production:    1907 - 1979;   production run of approx. 199,000
plus      variations
     1907 - 1947    (Police Positive)
     1907 - 1940    (Police Positive Target)
     1908 - 1979    (Police Positive Special)
     1926 - 1941    (Police Positive Target Model C); production
of        approx. 15,800
     1910 - 1925    (Police Positive Target Model G); production
of        approx. 242,000

Markings: Left side of barrel marked POLICE / POSITIVE, with only
one letter P (a large one) with OLICE and OSITIVE stacked next to
it. Target Models read same but have COLT prefix (on early ones)
and the word target as suffix. Second quality models have the
word PEQUANO on left side of barrel.

Barrel Lengths:2, 2.5, 4, 5, 6

Variations: Police Positive Target: early ones do not have
countersunk chambers in .22. Later models had countersunk
chambers in .22 starting around 1932. Checkered trigger,
elevation adjustable front sight, windage adjustable rear sight.
Police Positive Special: identical to regular Police Positive,
but has lengthened frame to accept longer cylinder needed for .38
Special ammo.

Notes: Probably the most confusing of the Colt’s, the Police
Positive family was, as it’s name implies, conceived and marketed
as a police weapon. In this regard it succeeded admirably and
many can be found bearing departmental markings. Target Models G
and C had their own serial number series, running parallel to the
one for the other Police Positive models. Pequano models are rare
finds.
