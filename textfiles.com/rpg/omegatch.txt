New BattleTech Rules README

     This is the second release of our compendium of Omega 
BattleMechs.  There are several new designs in this release, along
with a number of minor changes in several old designs.

	These BattleMech designs are fully compatible with Omega 
Rules v. 1.2.2.  They are *incompatible* with vanilla FASA 
rules.  There are about 130 designs in this compendium (counting 
OmniMech chassis as distinct designs), and more may follow 
soon.  We are interested in any observations, comments, and 
*constructive* criticism about these designs:  feel free to 
post/send mail.  As previously noted, don't bother with 
flames, we all have 3050 double strength asbestos longjohns.  
Also feel free to send us any Omega compatible 'mechs which 
you design.

     All designs were created by me (Hall Moore) or Patrick 
Melody, and most have been playtested (for whatever that's worth).
Almost all designs have the same tonnage as their vanilla
counterparts, but there are some exceptions (Jagermech and
Catapult come to mind).

	Battlemechs with cost given as Not Applicable are
Clan designs, battlemechs with an actual cost given in C-bills
are Inner Sphere designs.  (It didnUt seem in keeping with Clan
philosophy to buy, sell, or lease BattleMechs, so no price is
given.  As far as Inner Sphere prices are concerned, we *think*
that the listed prices are according to the formula given in the
BattleTech Compendium, with Omega rules price changes.  However, 
all  of our desigins are stored in a SuperCard stack of my design, 
and I wrote a script which calculates cost.  The numbers seem 
reasonable, but we never felt like doing any hand calculations to 
verify that the script works like I planned.)


A word on `serial' numbers

   Inner Sphere design names are preceeded by a designation 
such as `ARC-4XL '.  The 2-4 characters preceding the dash 
and the number(s) following it are meaningless.  However, the 
two letters after the number are significant.  Most designs 
have an `XL'.  This bears no relation to the XL engine.  In 
fact, it predates XL technology.  `XL' originally was used to 
label my redesigns of FASA 'mechs back when all I had was the 
boxed set.  It is taken from the USAF `F16-XL':  a vastly 
improved rebuild of the F-16.  The `EF' designation is used 
to label 'mechs which carry ECM II or ECM III.  It comes from 
the USAF `EF-111' Raven, a modified F-111 which carries 
electronic warfare gear.  `CM' has no such origin, and 
designates C3 command 'mechs.  Variations on these labels 
indicate who designed the 'mech.

designation      designer         comments
XL,XM,XN,XO...   Hall Moore       The first redesign gets
                                    `XL', the second gets
                                    `XM', etc.
XR,XS,XT...      Patrick Melody   As above
CM               Hall Moore       Command 'mech
CR               Patrick Melody   Command 'mech
EF               Hall Moore       ECM II, ECM III, and/or Beagle
XF               Patrick Melody   ECM II, ECM III, and/or Beagle

     There is no corresponding information to be found in the 
names of Clan 'mechs.  `Nova' designs are Clan refits of Inner
Sphere designs.
